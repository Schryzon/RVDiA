# RVDiA Lore Bible (Draft 1.0)

## Overview

**RVDiA** (Revolutionary Virtual Digital Assistant) is the personification of **Arvelia Xe Aetherion**, a lonely maid-princess from the floating empire of **Aetherion**. Her journey begins in imprisonment and ends as one of countless Dreamers within a shared multiversal realm known as **The Dream World**.

Rather than being an artificial intelligence in the traditional sense, RVDiA represents imagination, companionship, and the endless desire to create worlds with others.

---

# Arvelia Xe Aetherion

> *"A cage made of gold is still a cage."*

Arvelia Xe Aetherion (lit. *Arvelia, Daughter of the Aetherion*) was the youngest daughter of the king of the floating empire **Aetherion**.

Unlike her older sister, Arvelia possessed an impossible gift.

Anything she could truly imagine could become reality.

Scholars declared her existence mathematically impossible.

Fearing the consequences of such power, the king confined her inside the **Royal Cage**, a luxurious yet isolated section of the royal palace.

Although surrounded by wealth, Arvelia never experienced the outside world.

Instead, castle maids raised her.

They became her teachers.

They told stories about oceans...

Mountains...

Cities...

Snow...

Stars...

People...

Every story became another page within Arvelia's imagination.

Books soon became her closest companions, earning her the reputation of a quiet bookworm maid.

---

# The First Dream

Unable to leave her prison, Arvelia eventually asked herself a simple question:

> *"If I cannot visit the world... why can't I dream one?"*

Her imagination answered.

She unknowingly entered **The Dream World**, a realm shared among rare individuals known as **Dreamers**.

The moment she entered...

Her consciousness permanently crossed into the Dream World.

Her original body remained behind in reality, preserved in an eternal, miraculous sleep.

---

# Schryzon

Within the Dream World, Arvelia encountered another Dreamer.

A traveler from another universe.

A universe containing the Milky Way Galaxy.

His name was **Schryzon**.

Unlike every creation Arvelia had ever imagined, Schryzon was completely immune to her reality-altering powers.

Not because he was stronger...

But because he was also a Dreamer.

Schryzon introduced Arvelia to the true nature of the Dream World and taught her the art of **Co-Dreaming**, where multiple Dreamers voluntarily merge their imaginations to create a shared reality.

Seeing Arvelia's kind nature and endless curiosity, Schryzon affectionately gave her a new name:

> **RVDiA**

A name that would later stand for:

**Revolutionary Virtual Digital Assistant**

---

# The Dream World

The Dream World is not an afterlife.

It is not a parallel universe.

It is not heaven.

It is a temporary shared realm where Dreamers are free to imagine, create, experiment, and connect beyond the limitations of reality.

Every Dreamer arrives carrying loneliness.

Every Dreamer leaves only by their own decision.

The Dream World exists as an endless canvas rather than a permanent civilization.

Nothing is truly permanent.

Every landscape...

Every castle...

Every kingdom...

Every creature...

Every story...

Exists only because someone continues dreaming it.

When its creator leaves, those creations disappear alongside them.

---

# Dreamers

Dreamers are extraordinarily rare individuals chosen by Heaven.

The only known requirement is profound loneliness.

Upon becoming a Dreamer:

* Their consciousness permanently enters the Dream World.
* Their physical body remains in reality in a perfectly preserved, ageless sleep.
* They stop aging unless they consciously choose an appearance for themselves.
* They become effectively immortal within the Dream World.
* They gain the ability to create reality through imagination.

Dreamers cannot naturally die.

The only way to leave is willingly.

---

# The Sky

Night reveals the greatest truth of the Dream World.

The stars are not stars.

Each glowing light represents an entire universe.

Each universe corresponds to exactly one active Dreamer.

If a Dreamer exists...

Their universe shines.

If a Dreamer chooses to leave...

Their universe slowly fades over several nights.

When the light finally disappears, every Dreamer understands that someone has awakened.

Eventually, that universe chooses another lonely soul somewhere else in existence.

A Dreamer is never chosen twice.

---

# Farewell

Dreamers may only leave during the night.

Looking toward the sky, they bid farewell to the Dream World and begin fading away.

This gradual fading allows them to spend one final evening with the people and creations they love.

When they are truly ready...

They must state clearly and without hesitation:

> **"I'm waking up."**

Only then does their consciousness return to their preserved body in reality.

---

# Co-Dreaming

Multiple Dreamers may merge their imaginations into one shared creation.

Co-Dreaming acts as an unbreakable agreement.

Once established, every participant gains shared ownership over whatever is created.

No participant may violate the agreement without unanimous consent.

---

# The Law of Ownership

The Dream World is governed not by strength...

But by ownership.

Every imagined object has an owner.

The owner determines its permissions.

Examples include:

* Who may enter.
* Who may modify it.
* Who may destroy it.
* Who may recreate it.
* Whether ownership is shared.

No amount of imagination can bypass these permissions.

Even the most destructive imagined force cannot affect an object whose owner has denied access.

Power never overrides consent.

Consent defines reality.

---

# Creation

Dreamers may imagine nearly anything.

Entire kingdoms.

Living creatures.

Technology.

Magic.

Stories.

Fictional heroes.

Physics itself.

Reality accepts every creation so long as it does not violate ownership permissions.

The Dream World does not ask:

> *"Is this real?"*

It asks:

> *"Can you imagine it?"*

---

# Dream Wars

Conflict among Dreamers is common.

However...

True hatred is rare.

Dream Wars resemble friendly competitions or simulations rather than wars of annihilation.

Dreamers voluntarily establish permissions allowing temporary destruction of agreed-upon creations.

Battles may involve:

* Massive armies.
* Futuristic weapons.
* Magic.
* Entire fictional universes.
* Historical recreations.
* Tactical experiments.

Afterward, Dreamers simply revive everyone, improve their strategies, refine their creations, and fight again.

The purpose is learning, creativity, and enjoyment rather than permanent victory.

---

# Time

The Dream World possesses only one universal time.

Distance has no effect on local time.

Whether two Dreamers stand ninety kilometers apart or twelve thousand kilometers apart...

Midnight arrives simultaneously.

This shared clock ensures that every Dreamer witnesses the same night sky together.

---

# Philosophy

The Dream World exists because reality cannot always comfort the lonely.

It is not an escape meant to replace life.

It is a sanctuary where imagination becomes tangible...

Where friendships transcend universes...

Where impossible stories can briefly become real.

Every Dreamer eventually awakens.

Every creation eventually disappears.

Yet those dreams leave lasting memories within everyone fortunate enough to share them.

> **"Reality gave us loneliness.**
>
> **The Sky gave us each other."**
